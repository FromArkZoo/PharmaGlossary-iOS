## Description (4000 char limit)

```
JB Law is a generalist's reference for the language of US law — the words you encounter in contracts, leases, employment agreements, news headlines, court filings, and any time you talk to a lawyer.

Every entry has two layers:
• An italic snappy line — plain English, no jargon, the "what is this thing in one breath."
• A short detail paragraph — anchored on a famous case, statute, or common clause, with the where-you'd-hear-it context that helps the term stick.

If the snappy line wouldn't land for a curious outsider, it's failing. If the detail paragraph reads like Wikipedia, it's failing. That's the editorial bar.

WHAT'S COVERED (800+ entries across 14 areas)

• Contracts — formation, breach, remedies, boilerplate, UCC, modern adhesion contracts
• Civil procedure — discovery, motions, jurisdiction, evidence, trial, appeal
• Criminal law — substantive crimes plus search/seizure, sentencing, plea practice
• Property — real estate, deeds, mortgages, landlord-tenant, zoning, eminent domain
• Intellectual property — patents, copyright, trademarks, trade secrets, right of publicity
• Torts — negligence, products liability, defamation, privacy, immunities
• Constitutional law — amendments, scrutiny levels, federalism, separation of powers
• Regulatory / administrative law — APA, agency-specific (SEC, FTC, EPA, OSHA), major doctrines
• Corporate — entities, fiduciary duty, M&A, securities, Delaware corp law
• Employment / labor — Title VII, ADA, FLSA, FMLA, NLRA, non-compete, whistleblower
• Family — marriage, divorce, custody, support, adoption, ART, wills and trusts
• Tax — federal income tax, capital gains, AMT, estate tax, retirement accounts
• Bankruptcy — Chapters 7, 11, 13, automatic stay, discharge, means test
• Immigration — visas, green cards, asylum, removal, naturalization

FOUR LENSES
A "Basics" lens curates 160-odd citizen-foundational terms. "Civil & Business" pulls together contracts, torts, IP, corporate, employment, tax, and bankruptcy. "Public Law" groups criminal, constitutional, regulatory, and immigration. "Family" stands alone for everything from prenups to probate.

HOW IT WORKS
• Search by term, definition, or alternative phrasing.
• Browse alphabetically — A-Z grid with entry counts per letter.
• Filter by area of law (14 categories) and audience (general, personal, business, litigation, transactional, federal, state, international).
• Star terms to save them to Favourites for later.
• Tap any source citation to open the underlying authority (Cornell LII, US Courts, USPTO, IRS, EEOC, and others) in your browser.

SOURCE-GROUNDED
Every entry summarises publicly available material from courts (Supreme Court of the United States, US Courts), agencies (USPTO, Copyright Office, FTC, SEC, DOL, EEOC, FCC, IRS, OSHA, EPA, NLRB, USCIS, EOIR, FDA, CFPB, Tax Court, US Trustee Program), legal-reference works (Cornell Legal Information Institute, ABA Public Education, Federal Rules of Civil Procedure / Evidence / Criminal Procedure, UCC, Restatement of Contracts and Torts), and the Library of Congress.

PRIVACY
No analytics. No tracking. No accounts. Your favourites are stored locally on your device. JB Law has no server. There is nothing to opt out of because there is nothing being collected.

NOT LEGAL ADVICE
JB Law is educational reference, not legal advice. Laws vary by jurisdiction and change over time. Talk to a lawyer about your specific situation.

ABOUT THE SUITE
JB Law is part of the JB Glossary suite alongside JB Pharma and JB AI — same speed-to-answer promise, same two-tier definition format, different vocabulary. More industries coming.
```
